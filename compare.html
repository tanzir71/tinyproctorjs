tinyproctor.js vs. other exam proctoring approaches

A practical comparison focused on what’s realistically possible in a normal browser context. Client-side signals are indicators, not proof.

Comparison matrix

Different tools optimize for different threat models: deterrence, detection, or prevention.

Dimension tinyproctor.js Webcam proctoring Lockdown browser LMS-only logs Human in-room
Primary control Browser event signals + heartbeat. Video/audio monitoring + review. Enforcement via a controlled exam app/browser. Platform telemetry (navigation, timing, submissions). Physical supervision.
Signals Visibility, blur/focus, fullscreen exits, DevTools heuristics. Camera/mic, identity checks, environment scan (varies). App-level controls + device restrictions (varies). Limited to what the LMS records. High-fidelity, contextual observation.
Invasiveness Low: no camera/screen capture by default. High: camera/mic access and storage. Medium–high: strong restrictions; may require install. Low: minimal beyond the LMS. Medium: depends on setting.
Deployment Script tag + self-hosted collector. Vendor onboarding + policies + review workflow. Install/launch a controlled client. Use existing platform features. Scheduling + staffing.
Bypass resistance Low–medium: deters casual misuse; not bypass-proof. Medium: still bypassable with hardware/virtualization tricks. Higher: prevents many actions, still not absolute. Low: limited evidence. Highest in practice, within constraints.

Treat client-side “devtools detected” style events as heuristics. Use layered controls and human review for decisions.

Implications

What this tool is designed to do (and not do).

Best for

  • Low-friction monitoring signals for custom quiz/exam systems.
  • Heartbeat-based “active” tracking and basic integrity scoring.
  • Self-hosted environments with strict privacy requirements.

Not a replacement for

  • Secure browsers or OS-level enforcement.
  • Identity verification and high-assurance proctoring.
  • Proof of cheating based on a single client-side event.
Compare page for proctoring tradeoffs. Use multiple layers for high-stakes assessments.
